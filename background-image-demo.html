The Meaning of Flowers

Flowers hold many different meanings for each person but flowers also have offical meanings. There are so many beautiful folwers out there you could give someone to convey how you feel. So, to help you speak through your bouquets, this website tells you the meaning of different flowers.

Red Flowers

Aloe

Aloe flowers generally have both negative and positive connotation. The Aloe flowers are associated with affection, along with grief so be careful giving them as a gift.

Red Camellias

Camellias themselves have the meaning of love and affection. Red camellias in particular mean the phrase "You're a flame in my heart" so they make for great romantic gifts.

Red Chrysanthemums

Chrysanthemums are another flower that many people link to love. Red Chrysanthemums in particular are a perfect gift for your significant other as they mean "I love you".

Dahilas

Dahilas have a relatively simple meaning compared to the many other flowers of similar color. Dahilas mean good taste meaning they don't make the most romantic gift.

Red Roses

Red Roses are a common flower to gift the someone's significant other. This is because, like red chrysanthemums, Red Roses mean love or "I love you".

Red Salvias

Red is a very romantic color as you have seen from the many flowers before. Red Salvias follow that theme and have the beautiful meaning of "Forever mine".

Orange Flowers

Butterfly Weed

Butterfly Weed, though very pretty don't have the most romantic, or even postive meaning. Butterfly Weed menas "Let me go", meaning, if you don't plan to break up with your partner, aviod giving them these flowers.

Wallflower

Wallflower is a beautiful orange flower with an equally beautiful meaning. Wallflowers are linked to the phrase "Faithfulness in adversity" which meanings being together even in the face of difficulties.

Zinnia

The Zinnia flower is another gorgeous orange flower but it doesn't make for the best gift. Zinnia flowers mean "Thoughts of absent friends" which means they could be a nice gift to give an old friend.

Yellow Flowers

Black-eyed Susan

Back-eyed Susans have the lovely meaning of justice which fits the flowers yellow color. Though they might have a nice meaning, justice isn't the most romantic.

Yellow Chrysanthemums

Yellow Chrysanthemums are very unlike their red colored counterpart. Yellow Chrysanthemums have a very negative connotation and are linked to disdain, disappoinment, and rejection.

Daffodil

Daffodils are a lovely gift to give to your partner or a close loved one. Daffodils mean unequalled love, making them a perfect flower to give to the ones you love.

Fennel

Fennel are a little more green than yellow, with the flowers being particularly small. Even though they are not the most pretty flower, Fennel means flattery, so adding a little to your bouquet will make a great gift.

Yellow Hyacinth

All Hyacinth flowers mean sport, game, or play, positive and playful but the yellow color really changes that meaning. Yeloow Hyacinths area symbol of jealousy, a differnet meaning from other Hyacinth flowers.

Marigold

Marigold is yet another flower on the beautiful but negative list. Marigolds mean despair, grief, and jealousy, so this doesn't make for the best gift for anyone you might like.

Yellow Roses

Roses are a common flower to gift to others but yellow flowers are not the best color for it. Yellow roses are another flower which mean jealousy, but the flower also means a decrease of love and infidelity.

Yarrow

Though many of the yellow flowers so far have had negative meanings, Yarrow doesn't fit that mold. Yarrow means everlasting love, making it a great gift to give to your loved ones.

Dwarf Sunflowers

Everyone loves Sunflowers, they hold beauty and their image always reminds us of happiness. This appriciation for Sunflowers is well founded since Dwarf Sunflowers mean adoration.

Blue Flowers

Bluebells

Bluebells are lovely flowers that make even lovelier gifts due to thier kind meaning. Bluebells are associated with humility and constancy, making them good gifts for someone you want to be with for awhile.

Forget-me-not

Forget-me-nots have a meaning very similar to their name. Forget-me-nots of course mean "Do not forget me", but they also mean "True love memories" making them great gifting flowers.

Blue Hyacinth

So far we've seen what yellow Hyacinth mean, but what do Blue Hyacinths stand for? Blue Hyacinth are also very similar to Bluebells as they also mean constancy.

Blue Salvias

Like Blue Hyacinths, we've seen what a different colored Salvia means. Compared to red Salvias, Blue Salvias are a little less romantic but no less meaningful as they mean "I think of you".

Purple Flowers

Aster

Asters' beautiful purple color really reflect the message that the flower represents. Asters are a symbol of love and daintiness, making them lovely gifts to give.

Bittersweet

Bittersweet flower's meaning really relfects its name. Bittersweet flowers stand for truth which can be good, bad, or bittersweet!

Clematis

Clematis have the meaning of mental beauty. This means that they can make a lovely gift to give to someone you think has an amazing mind.

Spring Crocus

Spring is the season of beginnings, of youth and Spring Crocuses are no different. Spring Crocus flowers have the meaning of youthful gladness, making them a joyful gift.

Purple Hyacinths

Another Hyacinth, another color, another meaning. Purple Hyacinths are a but more negative meaning compared with other purple flowers so far as they mean sorrow.

Iris

Iris is flower that shares a name with a messanger goddess who the flower most likely get its name from. Iris flowers represent a message, for what is up to you.
